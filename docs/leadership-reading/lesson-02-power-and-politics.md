## 📝 Reading (~5 min)

Where does a leader's power come from? French and Raven's (1959) classic model identified **five bases of power** — and understanding them is essential for any leader.

### The Five Bases of Power

#### 1. Legitimate Power 🏛️
Power from your **position or title**.
- Example: The CEO can set company policy
- **Limitation:** It only works within your official scope

#### 2. Coercive Power ⚠️
Power from the **ability to punish**.
- Example: A manager can issue a warning or reduce bonus
- **Limitation:** Creates compliance, not commitment — breeds resentment

#### 3. Reward Power 🏆
Power from the **ability to give rewards**.
- Example: Promotions, raises, recognition, good assignments
- **Limitation:** People only follow when the reward is visible

#### 4. Expert Power 🎓
Power from **knowledge and skills**.
- Example: A senior engineer whose technical judgment is trusted
- **Strength:** Earned, not assigned — very durable

#### 5. Referent Power ⭐
Power from **being admired or respected**.
- Example: A charismatic leader people want to follow
- **Strength:** The most powerful form — creates loyalty and inspiration

### The Power Model

```
                    LEGITIMATE
                        ↓
REWARD ——— COERCIVE ———→ INFLUENCE ←——— EXPERT
                        ↑
                   REFERENT
```

Research shows that **Expert** and **Referent** power produce the best long-term results. Legitimate, Reward, and Coercive power are necessary but insufficient alone.

### Influence Strategies

How do leaders actually use power? Different situations call for different strategies:

| Strategy | What it means | When to use |
|----------|---------------|-------------|
| **Rational persuasion** | Use logic and facts | When the audience is analytical |
| **Inspirational appeals** | Appeal to values and emotions | When you need commitment, not just compliance |
| **Consultation** | Ask for input and participation | When buy-in matters |
| **Ingratiation** | Build goodwill before asking | When you need a favor |
| **Personal appeals** | Use friendship or loyalty | With close colleagues |
| **Exchange** | Trade favors or resources | When both sides benefit |
| **Coalition tactics** | Get others to support your case | When facing resistance |
| **Pressure** | Use demands or threats | Only as a last resort |
| **Legitimating** | Cite rules or authority | When your position is questioned |

### The Effective Leader's Approach

1. **Build expert power** → Stay knowledgeable in your field
2. **Cultivate referent power** → Build genuine relationships
3. **Use rational persuasion first** → Facts win arguments
4. **Use inspirational appeals when you need buy-in** → Connect to values
5. **Avoid coercion unless necessary** → It damages relationships

## 🔑 Key Vocabulary

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Base** | The foundation or source |
| **Legitimate** | Lawful, official, recognized |
| **Coercive** | Using force or threats |
| **Compliance** | Following orders (not necessarily agreeing) |
| **Commitment** | Believing in and owning the goal |
| **Resentment** | Bitter anger from unfair treatment |
| **Charismatic** | Exceptionally charming and influential |
| **Ingratiation** | Winning favor through flattery |
| **Coalition** | A temporary alliance of people |
