# 📖 Day 3: Leadership Traits & Skills

**Source:** "Nghệ thuật Lãnh đạo" — Chapter 4: Traits & Skills Approach

## 📝 Reading (~5 min)

Is leadership something you're born with? The **Trait Approach** believes leaders have certain innate qualities. The **Skills Approach** argues effective leadership can be learned.

### The Trait Approach

Early research (Stogdill, 1948; Mann, 1959) tried to identify universal leadership traits. Later studies found these **five key traits** are most consistently linked to leadership:

#### 1. Intelligence 🧠
Not just IQ — it's **practical intelligence**: the ability to think critically, solve problems, and make sound decisions in real situations.

#### 2. Self-Confidence 💪
Leaders who believe in themselves inspire others to believe in them. Confident leaders:
- Take calculated risks
- Make decisions without excessive hesitation
- Handle setbacks resiliently

#### 3. Determination 🎯
The drive to get things done. Determined leaders are:
- Proactive — they act, not react
- Persistent — they don't quit at the first obstacle
- Dominant — they take charge when needed

#### 4. Integrity 🛡️
The quality of being honest and having strong moral principles. Leaders with integrity:
- Keep promises
- Are transparent about decisions
- Earn trust over time

#### 5. Sociability 🤝
The ability to build relationships. Sociable leaders:
- Communicate well
- Are diplomatic and tactful
- Build networks easily

### The Skills Approach (Katz, 1955)

Robert Katz argued that effective leadership depends on **three core skills** rather than personality:

```
                   TOP         MIDDLE     FRONT-LINE
                 MANAGEMENT   MANAGEMENT   MANAGEMENT
                    
CONCEPTUAL         HIGH         MODERATE      LOW
SKILLS
(Strategy, vision,
systems thinking)

HUMAN              HIGH         HIGH         HIGH
SKILLS
(Communication,
empathy, teamwork)

TECHNICAL          LOW         MODERATE       HIGH
SKILLS
(Domain expertise,
tools, methods)
```

#### Conceptual Skills 🧩
- Seeing the organization as a whole
- Strategic thinking — how parts fit together
- **Most important at senior levels**

#### Human Skills 👥
- Working effectively with people
- Communication, empathy, motivation
- **Equally important at ALL levels**

#### Technical Skills 🔧
- Knowledge of specific methods and tools
- Deep expertise in your field
- **Most important at front-line/supervisor levels**

### Modern View: It's Both

Today, leadership scholars agree:
- **Traits give you potential** (the raw material)
- **Skills are developed through experience** (the craft)
- **The best leaders combine both**

> *"Leaders are made, not born, and they are made just like anything else — through hard work."* — Vince Lombardi

## 🔑 Key Vocabulary

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Innate** | Inborn, natural (not learned) |
| **Trait** | A distinguishing quality or characteristic |
| **Resilient** | Able to recover quickly from difficulties |
| **Proactive** | Taking initiative, acting in advance |
| **Persistent** | Continuing firmly despite obstacles |
| **Transparent** | Open, honest, no hidden agenda |
| **Diplomatic** | Tactful and sensitive in dealing with others |
| **Conceptual** | Relating to abstract ideas |
| **Empathy** | The ability to understand others' feelings |
| **Domain** | An area of knowledge or activity |
