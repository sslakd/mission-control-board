# 📖 Day 1: What Is Leadership?

**Source:** "Nghệ thuật Lãnh đạo" — Chapter 1: Definitions of Leadership

---

## 📝 Reading (~5 min)

Leadership is a complex concept that scholars have defined in many ways over the past decades. Here are key definitions that shaped our understanding:

### Classic Definitions

> *"Leadership is the behavior of an individual when he directs the activities of a group toward a shared goal."* — Hemphill & Coons (1957)

> *"Leadership is a special form of power relationship characterized by group members' perception that another member has the right to prescribe certain behaviors."* — Janda (1960)

> *"Leadership is an influence process, carried out in a situation, directed through communication toward achieving specific goals."* — Tannenbaum, Weschler & Masarik (1961)

### A Modern Synthesis

Most scholars agree on this core idea:

**Leadership is a process where an individual influences others toward achieving group or organizational goals.**

Key elements:
- **Process** — not a position, but an ongoing activity
- **Influence** — the ability to shape others' thoughts and actions
- **Goal achievement** — it's purposeful, not random
- **Group context** — needs followers, not a solo act

### Manager vs. Leader

Abraham Zaleznik (Harvard Business Review, 1986) drew a famous distinction:

| Manager | Leader |
|---------|--------|
| Maintains existing operations | Creates new directions |
| Works within the system | Challenges the system |
| Focuses on efficiency | Focuses on vision |
| Avoids risk | Embraces calculated risk |
| Problem-solves | Inspires |

But John Kotter (1990) argues: **Organizations need BOTH.**
- **Management** brings order and consistency
- **Leadership** produces change and movement

> *"Leadership and management are different and complementary systems, each with its own function and activities. Both are necessary for managing complex organizations."* — John Kotter

### The Leadership Process Cycle

```
Leader sets direction
        ↓
    Communicates vision
        ↓
    Provides resources
        ↓
    Coaches & supports
        ↓
    Empowers followers
        ↓
   Customer satisfaction
        ↗           ↖
   Feedback loop — what works? what needs change?
```
